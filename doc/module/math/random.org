#+OPTIONS: toc:nil num:nil ^:nil
#+STARTUP: showall

* Purpose

Geometric random samplers that produce math types: =unit_vector2= /
=unit_vector3= (uniform directions), =point_in_unit_disc= / =point_in_unit_ball=
(uniform points inside the circle / sphere), and =random_angle=. They are layered
on the [[file:../random/README.org][nexenne::random]] engines and reuse that module's scalar draws rather than
re-deriving them.

* The idea

Scalar sampling (uniform reals and ints, Bernoulli, normal) is the job of the
engine-agnostic =nexenne::random= module. What belongs *here* is the handful of
samplers that return a [[file:vector.org][vector]] or a [[file:angle.org][radians]] and therefore need the math types -
so they live next to those types but still take any =random::rng_engine= as the
source of bits. Every sampler advances the caller's generator in place; the
generator owns the sequence state.

The subtlety in all of these is *uniformity*. The obvious constructions are
biased: sampling a radius and angle independently clusters points near a disc's
center, and sampling spherical angles clusters directions near the poles. The
samplers here avoid that, either by rejection or by a measure-preserving map.

* How it works

** uniform_real_in (the shared primitive)

A small =detail= helper remaps the engine's canonical =[0, 1)= draw onto
=[lo, hi)= with =lo + (hi - lo) * uniform_real(g)=. Everything else is built from
it, so there is exactly one place the underlying engine is touched.

** unit_vector2

Sample an angle uniformly over a full turn and take its point on the unit circle.
=sincos= returns both components from a single trig evaluation. Uniform angle maps
directly to uniform direction in 2D, so no rejection is needed.

** unit_vector3 - Marsaglia's method

A uniform direction in 3D cannot come from uniform spherical angles (that biases
toward the poles). Marsaglia (1972) instead rejection-samples a point =(a, b)= in
the unit disc, sets =s = a^2 + b^2=, and maps to
=(2a*sqrt(1-s), 2b*sqrt(1-s), 1-2s)=. This is *exactly* uniform on the sphere by
Archimedes' hat-box theorem: a uniform sphere has its =z= uniform on =[-1, 1]= and
its azimuth uniform. For a uniform disc point =s= is uniform on =[0, 1)= (disc
area grows as the squared radius), so =z = 1-2s= is the required uniform =z=; and
since =1 - z^2 = 4s(1-s)=, the in-plane radius =sqrt(1-z^2) = 2*sqrt(s(1-s))=
reproduces the disc point scaled onto its circle of latitude. Two draws and one
=sqrt= per accepted sample, no trigonometry, acceptance =pi/4= (~1.27 attempts).

** point_in_unit_disc and point_in_unit_ball

Plain rejection sampling: draw a point in the bounding square (2D) or cube (3D)
and keep it only if it lands inside the unit radius. This is unbiased by
construction - the kept points are a uniform subset of a uniform square/cube.
Acceptance is =pi/4= (~1.27 attempts) for the disc and =pi/6= (~1.91 attempts)
for the ball, both cheap enough that the simplicity wins.

** random_angle

A uniform =radians= in =[-pi, pi)=, sampled directly in range (no wrapping).

* When to use it

- =unit_vector3= for a random ray direction, a scatter direction in a path
  tracer, or jitter on a normal.
- =point_in_unit_disc= for sampling a lens aperture (depth of field) or a 2D
  splatter; =point_in_unit_ball= for volumetric scatter.
- =random_angle= for a random 2D heading or spin.
- Reach for [[file:../random/README.org][nexenne::random]] directly for scalar draws (a uniform float, a normal
  sample, a dice roll); this header is only the geometric layer on top.

* Usage

#+BEGIN_SRC cpp
namespace nm = nexenne::math;
namespace rng = nexenne::random;

rng::pcg32 gen{seed, sequence};
auto const dir{nm::unit_vector3<double>(gen)};        // uniform on the sphere
auto const lens{nm::point_in_unit_disc<double>(gen)};  // uniform in the disc
auto const heading{nm::random_angle<double>(gen)};     // radians in [-pi, pi)
#+END_SRC
