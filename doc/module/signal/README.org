#+TITLE: nexenne::signal
#+OPTIONS: toc:nil num:nil ^:nil
#+STARTUP: showall

A header-only signal/slot system: a typed, multicast callback channel in the
Qt sense, built on [[file:../utility/README.org][nexenne::utility]] and [[file:../container/README.org][nexenne::container]]. The single idea that
ties the module together: a =signal<R(Args...)>= is a list of callables (slots)
that you connect to and then fire all at once with =emit=, with no virtual
dispatch and no heap on the slot path. Type erasure uses
=utility::in_place_function= (fixed inline storage, a callable too large is
rejected at compile time) and the slot list is a =container::small_vector= with
four inline slots, so a typical signal with a handful of subscribers allocates
only once, for its shared core. That makes it usable in an embedded event loop,
not just on a desktop.

The hard parts a naive callback list gets wrong are lifetime and reentrancy, and
those are the module's focus: a subscriber can outlive or predecease the signal,
a slot can connect or disconnect (itself or others) or even destroy the signal
from inside its own callback, and emits can nest. Every one of those is defined
and safe here.

* The emitter

- [[file:signals.org][signals]] : =signal= and its connect-only =sink= view. The connect overloads
  (plain, =connect_once=, member-function, lifetime-tracked), priority ordering,
  =emit= and =emit_and_collect=, =block= / =unblock=, and the reentrancy and
  destroy-during-emit guarantees.

* The handles

- [[file:connections.org][connections]] : the lifetime tools. =connection= and the RAII
  =scoped_connection= returned by =connect=, the receiver-side =slot= that
  auto-disconnects every subscription a subscriber makes when it dies, and the
  RAII =emit_blocker= that suppresses emission for a scope.

* The heap-free variant

- [[file:static.org][static_signal]] : the allocation-free, fixed-capacity sibling of =signal=, the
  =static_vector= to =signal='s =small_vector=. All state inline, zero heap, an
  =emit= that never allocates, in exchange for a bounded slot count and token
  connections that must not outlive the signal.
